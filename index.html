Dr. Manuel Elkin Patarroyo

A man who found a Vaccine

Here's a time line of Dr. Manuel's life:

  • 1914 - Born in chicago, Illinois
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Chicago, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Military Service
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 69 years Margret Gibson. Gets laid off due to budget cuts. Inspired by Charles Dike, he returns to school study under Stakman, who teaches him about breeding pest-resistent plants.
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the military after the Pearl Harbor attack, but is rejected. Instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D. in Microbiology Pathology
